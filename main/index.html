Hi, I’m Kostas 👋

Konstantinos (Kostas) N. Plataniotis received his B. Eng. degree in Computer Engineering from the University of Patras, Greece and his M.S. and Ph.D. degrees in Electrical Engineering from Florida Institute of Technology Melbourne, Florida. Dr. Plataniotis is currently a Professor with The Edward S. Rogers Sr. Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering at the University of Toronto in Toronto, Ontario, Canada, where he directs the Multimedia Laboratory. He has held the Bell Canada Endowed Chair in Multimedia since 2014. His research interests primarily include image/signal processing, machine learning and adaptive learning systems, visual data analysis, multimedia and knowledge media, and affective computing. Dr. Plataniotis is a Fellow of IEEE, a Fellow of the Engineering Institute of Canada, a Fellow of the Canadian Academy of Engineering / L’ Academie Canadienne Du Genie, and a registered professional engineer in Ontario.

Color Image Processing and Applications

Konstantinos N. Plataniotis, and A. N. Venetsanopoulos

"Color Image Processing and Applications" is a book that reports on the state of the art of color image processing. It covers a gap in the literature on digital signal and image processing and contains numerous examples and pictures of color image processing results, as well as a library of algorithms implemented in C. The book was authored by Konstantinos N. Plataniotis and Anastasios N. Venetsanopoulos and was published by Springer in 2000. It is a good reference text for color image processing up to that time.

Multilinear Subspace Learning: Dimensionality Reduction of Multidimensional Data

Haiping Lu, Konstantinos N. Plataniotis, and A. N. Venetsanopoulos

"Multilinear Subspace Learning: Dimensionality Reduction of Multidimensional Data" is a book co-authored by Haiping Lu, K. N. Plataniotis, and A. N. Venetsanopoulos, published by Taylor and Francis in December 2013. The book is part of the Chapman & Hall/CRC Press Machine Learning and Pattern Recognition Series and provides a comprehensive survey of the field of multilinear subspace learning. The book covers various algorithms for dimensionality reduction of multidimensional data, which can be represented as tensors. The book includes sample chapters, a table of contents, and previews of the content. The authors also provide open-source software and data for multilinear subspace learning algorithms. Additionally, the book includes information on the FERET face data, CMU PIE face data, and USF gait data. The book is recommended for researchers and students interested in tensor data analysis, and it is suitable for those with elementary knowledge of probability theory, statistics, and linear algebra.
